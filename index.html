Jonathan Mah

I am a computational evolutionary biologist interested in the eco-evolutionary forces that shape genetic diversity in natural populations, with an emphasis on microbial populations. I am currently a PhD candidate at UCLA, working in the Garud Lab in UCLA's department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology, where I research the spatial distribution of genetic diversity in the gut microbiome, the dynamics of adaptation in the context of microbial invasion, and other topics.

I am also interested in science pedagogy. As a PhD student at UCLA, I have tought a variety of courses at UCLA on subjects ranging on population genetics to computational analysis of microbial sequencing data. I also have served as a mentor to undergraduate participating in the Bruins-In-Genomics (B.I.G) Summer Program and the Bioscience Scholars Program (BSP), two summer research experience for undergraduates (REU) programs hosted by UCLA, the latter which focuses on providing research experiences to students from underrepresented minority groups. I am passionate about using my position in academia to make STEM education more accessible to the next generation of potential scientists.
